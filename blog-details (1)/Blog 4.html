Posters

These posters were created to promote the fictional book Encyclopedia of Ancient Egypt: The Rameses Dynasty while capturing the rich visual identity of ancient Egyptian culture. The main goal was to design promotional material that immediately grabs attention while communicating the themes of history, mythology, and divine kingship.

The central focus of the posters is Pharaoh Ramses XIII, whose portrait serves as the main visual element. Placing the pharaoh at the center establishes him as the most important figure and creates an immediate connection with the audience.

Egyptian iconography was incorporated throughout the posters to strengthen the overall theme. Decorative hieroglyphic borders frame the composition, while symbols such as the cobra, the Eye of Horus, and the ankh reference protection, divine authority, and eternal life.
